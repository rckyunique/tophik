Mount Kilimanjaro Freequently Asked Questions

How fit would I have to be for climbing Mt Kilimanjaro & Meru?

People with age from 10 years of age and different fitness levels have a good chance to making the climb. To better equip our climbers, at Northland Adventures, we offer a Fitness Assessment and with the results, we will customize a Fitness Program/Regiment that is tailored to each individual’s lifestyle, health & fitness level.

What kind of gear would I need to climb Mt Kilimanjaro and Meru?
We have a specific packing/preparation list for each mountain trek. You can contact us directly for a copy of this list at info@northlandafricasafaris.com
What is the weather like?
In Kilimanjaro: Daytime temperatures range between 20⁰ - 27⁰ Celsius depending on the month. The sun can be very high on Kilimanjaro so it is important that you pack a high factor sun protection cream. Night time temperatures can range from 2⁰-3⁰ Celsius on the Shira Plateau, to as low as -10⁰. For those climbing in May there is the slight possibility of rain.
Are there toilet facilities on the mountain?
For both Mt Kilimanjaro and Meru, we would be providing chemical toilets, which are transported up the mountain by the team of porters. We also supply the group with toilet rolls and treated water. For Marangu Route (Mt Kilimanjaro), there will be public toilet available on the each camp.
How do I shower/clean myself on the mountain?
Each morning and evening, our climbers will receive a bowl of warm water and soap for washing. We also recommend packing baby wipes for regular cleaning purposes on each day.
What is the food like on the mountain?
We provide our climbers with carefully selected ingredients that will give you the required nutrients for an intense trek. We can also cater to all of your special dietary requirements for all your meals. Snacks such as muffins, cookies are also provided during breaks, paired with coffee or tea, or hot chocolate!

What about clean water on the trek?
All of the water we supply our climbers are treated and filtered using the Katadyn water system, which ensures top quality water daily for drinking and food preparation.
Is my health monitored throughout the trek?
A very careful approach is undertaken. We carry full medical supplies that could possibly be required from oxygen tanks to resuscitation equipment. Everyone is assessed daily, both morning and evening for; respiratory rate, heart rate, blood pressure and O2 saturation levels. We can also provide full medical support with a qualified expedition doctor, if needed, at additional costs per group.
Do I need other vaccinations?
Aside from Malaria and Yellow Fever, there may be other vaccination and medications to take prior to your trip to Tanzania. Please consult with your family doctor, and visit a travel clinic in your area to receive specific medical consultation before your trip. Please note that all shots and medications are recommended to be taken and prescribed at least 3-4 weeks prior to travel. Please discuss the following vaccination recommendations with your doctor or travel clinic:  Hepatitis A, B  Yellow Fever  Booster doses for Tetanus-diphtheria and polio vaccine  Anti-malarial drugs  Various antibiotics such as Cipro  Diarrhea medicine Contact us for more detail at info@northlandafricasafaris.com
